## ‚ö†Ô∏è RISKS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Shops Don't Want to Pay Commission
**Mitigation:**
- Start with low/no fees to prove value
- Show increased sales from platform
- Emphasize free delivery customers get
- Highlight professional online presence

### Risk 2: Low Adoption in Small Town
**Mitigation:**
- Target smartphone users first (growing in Kenya)
- Partner with M-Pesa agents for awareness
- Offer "offline ordering" via WhatsApp initially
- Focus on convenience for busy customers

### Risk 3: Competition from Bigger Players
**Mitigation:**
- Focus on local/hyper-local (Glovo doesn't cover Kutus)
- Personal relationships with shop owners
- Lower fees than big platforms
- Faster delivery (all shops are nearby)
- Swahili/local language support

### Risk 4: Delivery Logistics
**Mitigation:**
- Start with existing boda boda riders
- Commission-based (no fixed costs)
- Simple delivery zones (town center only initially)
- Partner with established boda associations

## üéì LESSONS FROM SUCCESSFUL MARKETPLACES

### What Makes Marketplaces Successful:

1. **Network Effects**
   - More shops = More variety = More customers
   - More customers = More sales = More shops join
   - Focus on growing both sides simultaneously

2. **Solve Real Pain Points**
   - Shops: Low foot traffic, no online presence
   - Customers: Inconvenient to visit multiple shops
   - You: Connect both and make commission

3. **Start Hyper-Local**
   - Glovo started in one city (Barcelona)
   - Jumia started with electronics only
   - You: Start with Kutus, master it first

4. **Quality Control**
   - Vet shops before allowing them
   - Monitor delivery times
   - Handle customer complaints quickly
   - Maintain platform reputation
